About learning (I).

by Husein

Instead of writing long post about learning, I will break this topic into three different posts that are first of all note to self, and hopefully in the future somebody else can find them useful to create his own learning system. This post was inspired by Marty Lobdell and his video lecture on 'Study Less, Study Smart' that I watched couple of years ago, before I even started learning coding and computer science. Below are some most valuable notes I have taken, and also a link for his YouTube lecture video.


The time we spend learning effectively is 25 minutes in total. After that we need to take a short break of 5 minutes to do something other than learning. If we do this in the course of 6 hours, we will get about 5.5 hours of serious studying and half an hour of break time. After those 6 hours of studying you want to give yourself a big treat. Whenever your studying time is over – plan something special.


Find the proper time to study. If you plan your day right, you can have those study breaks, and the coolest part is this – because you’re now reinforcing it with those little breaks doing something fun – you extend it, and we will find that we can go 30 - 50 minutes, or an hour without break. In his video Marty also mentioned 'true study' concept, basically a room where we are only learning or studying, not doing anything else. I think this is a non-sense because for me personally it's only important to have a room where I can be distraction free and focused on what I am learning.


The more active you are with your learning the more effective it will be. First decide what you are learning – is it a concept or a fact? A fact is a discrete little piece of information, example 5 = 5 is a fact, but understanding what is 5 is a concept. Or understanding the name of the bone is a fact, but understanding what it does in a body is a concept. It’s important to grasp a concept and once we do it it will stay with us for a lifetime. If you understand the concept you can quickly Google the fact if you need it. Break down the facts to understandable concepts.


Take notes. Highlight or write down the most important thing, it's a waste of time to write everything. How do you know that you know something? If you look at it, go to the next one and read it, and then stop and go back to the one before – look up in the sky and then in your own words say what that was about, if you succeed, you can be sure that you know it.


Taking notes is vital, we need to have in mind one simple rule – right after the class expand on everything that you jotted down during the class, give it depth, flesh it out. If you decide to do it a couple of hours after the lecture, you will forget most of the notes you’ve made. The best way to learn something is to teach it to someone else. Also, sleep is very much important. Around 8 hours is enough to get the REM that we need in order to memorize what we studied, or our permanent memories.
