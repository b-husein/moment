About learning (III).

by Husein

The third part of the post is related to learning from tutorials. It's crucial for people entering CS field, and also as note to self because I often tend to stuck in 'tutorial hell', in other words, start learning something new and leave it because something else has caught my attention. We all been there. In order to avoid tis problem, we should understand couple of things.


First, we need to know our 'why'. What is the purpose of tutorial and our expectations from it? What are we going to learn from it, and where can we apply the knowledge later? After answering these questions we will get clear picture about it, and it will not be hard for us to finish it because our 'why' is actually our motivation.


Second, add something of our own to tutorial projects, or if we want to go to the next level - make something of our own. Creating something by writing our own code is a master level because it shows that we understand how to use the code in practical way. It's always easier to modify code written (or already modified) by other programmers, but creating something of your own is whole another level.


Third, it's important to study documentation and go through the tutorial after that, or read an article or a book related to language you're already learning. After that we will be able to create something on our own, to write code with understanding of the concepts behind it.


Fourth, tutorials will give us conceptual knowledge related to some topic, but only way to actually learn something is by doing, practicing, learning from trial and error. If we are forced to use the knowledge, it's going to be hard and we will struggle from time to time, but it will get easier because we will know where to look for the solution next time (StackOverflow, of course).


Summa summarum, previous three posts are related to my learning process that, as everything else, evolves through time as I'm learning new and different, more productive concepts along the way.
